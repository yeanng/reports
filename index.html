The insight worth stealing

A friend asking beats a stranger, every time

Peer endorsement is the single most powerful trigger for giving. Cold approaches — phone calls, street asks, door knocks — are the fastest way to get a "no."

The gap = the opportunity

Even keen givers don't know how to give structurally

About half of these engaged individuals have little or no understanding of structured giving vehicles — foundations, sub-funds, ancillary funds. Not a lack of will. A lack of the map.

Where the money lands

So what?

Two opportunities the report points to

1

Bridge the education gap

People want to give well — they just don't know the vehicles. Even active givers need a clearer map to structured giving.

2

Make giving social & visible

Peer asks work. Sharing why you give — openly, on the platforms people already trust — moves more than any cold campaign.

"To give, and talk about giving, with pride, openness and encouragement. To not only give, but give intentionally and effectively — caring about impact." — A West Australian giver, on what a "giving culture" means
